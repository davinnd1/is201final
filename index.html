Get Started

The Manilla Phillipines Temple

What a place. Just across the street is the Phillipines Missionary Training Center. It was in these two places that I began to learn what was really important in this life. For the first time in my life I was really diving into the questions; why are we here, where did we come from, and where are we going. Over the course of the next 2 years I found answers to all three. A bit of knowledge that is invaluable. For the first time in my life I lost sight of Football, Trucks, Work, and everything else that won't matter 100 years from now and focused solely on Jesus Christ and being an instrument in bringing others unto him. In doing so I found more Joy and Happiness than I thought possible.

Trucking

Pictured left is, with minor adjustment, my dream semi. It's a Peterbilt 359. I got into trucking as a result of thinking really. My college football career and aspirations went from full swing to the garbage can in a hurry. When the dust had settled from that episode, I thought to myself, 'well I've always wanted to drive really big stuff.'' so then I said to myself 'I can either get my CDL and explore that dream or try and bump my first semester at BYU up to Fall 23' from Fall 24'' I decided to get my CDL. I got my first job hauling gas for Maverik, the youngest hire in the companies 28 year history, and it's been a blast. I don't think I'll ever want to completely get out of trucking.

Motorcycles

I recently purcharsed my first motorcycle, a Harley Davidson FXDR 114 same model, with a few differences from the one pictured here. I've always wanted a road bike, so against my parents wishes, I bought one. I love it, It's so much fun. There's nothing like getting in the wind with your buddies. It's just so free.

ATVs

My first love. Pictured here is a 1988 Honda 250R, it's been in the family longer than me. I've been on it ever since I can remember and I still ride it today. I love riding ATV's especially at the sand dunes. It's such a rush. It's especially fun for me to ride our 250Rs I wanted to for so long as a boy, to ride them just as good as dad is quite a joy to me.
